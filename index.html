Research Interests: My research focuses on creating reliable, trustworthy, and efficient AI systems. These efforts span from building theoretical foundations to delivering practical tools with real-world impact—particularly in science, society, and human-centered domains.

  1. Reliable AI Foundations: Detecting the Unexpected. I develop core algorithms to identify anomalies, outliers, and out-of-distribution (OOD) instances, enhancing both the robustness and trustworthiness of AI systems under unfamiliar or rare scenarios.
    Keywords: Anomaly Detection, Outlier Mining, OOD Detection, Robust AI, Trustworthy AI
  2. Graph Intelligence: Learning from Relational Structures. Extending foundational detection methods, I build graph-based algorithms to model and analyze complex relationships, enabling discovery in domains such as finance, science, and online platforms.
    Keywords: Graph Learning, Graph Outlier Detection, Open-set Learning, Structured Data Mining
  3. Foundation Models in Practice: From Insights to Societal Impact. By combining robust modeling with the expressiveness of large language models (LLMs) and generative AI, I explore critical applications in scientific discovery, political forecasting, and computational social science—bridging AI with the humanities and public policy.
    Keywords: LLMs, Generative AI, AI for Science, AI for Society, Political Forecasting, Computational Social Science
  4. Scalable, Open, and Automated AI Systems. To amplify the reach of these methods, I build scalable, reproducible, and open-source ML systems—such as PyOD (25M+ downloads). Collectively, my open-source projects have received over 20,000 GitHub stars, placing me in the top 800 globally by GitHub popularity.
    Keywords: Automated ML, Open-source AI, ML Systems, Distributed Computing
