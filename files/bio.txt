Dr. Yue Zhao is an Assistant Professor of Computer Science at the University of Southern California and a faculty member of the USC Machine Learning Center. He leads the FORTIS Lab (Foundations of Robust Trustworthy Intelligent Systems), where he builds robust, trustworthy, and scalable AI systems by addressing three interconnected challenges: developing Robust and Trustworthy AI Methods; applying LLM and Generative AI Applications; and creating Scalable, Automated, and Open-source ML Systems. His work spans AI4Science, healthcare, finance, and political science. Dr. Zhao has authored over 50 papers in top-tier venues and is recognized for his open-source contributions—including PyOD, PyGOD, TDC, and TrustLLM—which collectively have garnered over 20,000 GitHub stars and 25 million downloads. His projects have been adopted by leading organizations such as NASA, Morgan Stanley, and the U.S. Senate Committee on Homeland Security & Governmental Affairs. Dr. Zhao has received numerous awards, including the Capital One Research Awards, AAAI New Faculty Highlights Award, Google Cloud Research Innovators, Norton Fellowship, Meta AI4AI Research Award, and the CMU Presidential Fellowship. He also serves as an associate editor for IEEE Transactions on Neural Networks and Learning Systems (TNNLS), an action editor for the Journal of Data-centric Machine Learning Research (DMLR), and as an area chair for leading machine learning conferences.
