Dr. Yue Zhao is an Assistant Professor of Computer Science at the University of Southern California and a faculty member of the USC Machine Learning Center. He directs the FORTIS Lab (Foundations Of Robust Trustworthy Intelligent Systems), where his research focuses on developing robust, efficient, and automated AI systems, particularly in the areas of anomaly detection, out-of-distribution (OOD) detection, automated ML, ML systems, and AI applications in security, finance, and healthcare. Dr. Zhao has contributed over 40 papers to leading venues and is renowned for his open-source ML systems, including PyOD, PyGOD, TDC, and TrustLLM, which have gained over 20,000 GitHub stars and 23 million downloads. His projects are used by organizations such as NASA, Morgan Stanley, and the U.S. Senate Committee on Homeland Security & Governmental Affairs. He has received recognition including the Amazon Research Awards, AAAI New Faculty Highlights Award, Google Cloud Research Innovators, Norton Fellowship, Meta AI4AI Research Award, and CMU Presidential Fellowship. Dr. Zhao serves as an associate editor for IEEE Transactions on Neural Networks and Learning Systems (TNNLS), an action editor for the Journal of Data-centric Machine Learning Research (DMLR), a workflow co-chair for KDD 2023, and an area chair for several ML conferences.
