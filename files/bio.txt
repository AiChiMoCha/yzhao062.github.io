Dr. Yue Zhao is an Assistant Professor of Computer Science at the University of Southern California and a faculty member of the USC Machine Learning Center. His research advances machine learning (ML) and data mining, focusing on AI robustness and security through anomaly and out-of-distribution (OOD) detection, efficient and scalable AI systems, and applications in security, finance, and healthcare. His recent work explores the intersection of foundation models and generative AI with anomaly and OOD detection. Dr. Zhao has contributed over 40 papers to leading venues and is renowned for his open-source ML systems. He has created more than 10 projects garnering over 20,000 GitHub stars and 22 million downloads. His notable projects, including PyOD, PyGOD, TDC, and TrustLLM, are utilized by organizations such as NASA, Morgan Stanley, and the U.S. Senate Committee on Homeland Security & Governmental Affairs for high-stakes applications. Dr. Zhao has received recognition including Amazon Research Awards, AAAI New Faculty Highlights Award, Google Cloud Research Innovators, Norton Fellowship, Meta AI4AI Research Award, and CMU Presidential Fellowship. He serves as an associate editor for IEEE Transactions on Neural Networks and Learning Systems (TNNLS), an action editor for the Journal of Data-centric Machine Learning Research (DMLR), a workflow co-chair for KDD 2023, and an area chair for numerous ML conferences.
